Ireland is an island in the North Atlantic Ocean, on one of the two main British Isles off the coast of Western Europe. The island of Ireland is the twentieth largest island in the world at 32,595 square miles and peaks at 3,415 feet above sea level. The island is politically divided into two domains: the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland, which is part of the United Kingdom. This current division was established after the signing of the Anglo-Irish Treaty in 1921. The main languages ​​spoken in the country are English, Irish and Scottish Ulster. Dublin is the largest and capital city of the Republic of Ireland, with a population of approximately 1.5 million.

An unofficial number estimates Ireland has over 1,000 castles. Scattered across the country, most of the medieval castles were built as defensive by the island's most powerful families. Unfortunately, most of the castles have been abandoned and left to lie like ruins. However, many of the fortified palaces have been restored to their former glory and can be explored by visitors.

The Wild Atlantic Way is the longest coastal road in the world, 2,500 km long and runs along the west coast of Ireland along the Atlantic Ocean. Wild nature, charming country houses blended in with scenery untouched by the progress of civilization, hundreds of coves, monumental cliffs, beautiful mountains, majestic islets and it's hard to believe that it is so close in Europe, not somewhere on the edge of the world. People live differently here, time flows differently and you don't want to leave.
